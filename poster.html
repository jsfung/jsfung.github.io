CHALLENGE

Type is a critical building block for communication, and the foundation of good typographic design is a strong relationship between the visual and verbal aspects it holds.

The challenge of this assignment was to interpret content in order to shape the design of a lecture poster, using the same grid, but modifying the placement of elements and typographic hierarchy. Each iteration was created in Adobe InDesign.

ITERATIONS

There were four iterations performed in creating the final lecture poster: (1) plain text, (2) use of bold and italic, (3) use of additional typeface and size, and (4) wild cards. Through each iteration, I aimed to have a single point of entry and have progressive disclosure take place in revealing sections of information at a time.

Iteration One: plain text

Iteration Two: use of bold and italic

Iteration Three: use of additional typeface and size

Iteration Four: wild card

OUTCOME

The final design brought the focal point to Don Norman's name, followed by the time and admission information, followed by the title of each lecture. After capturing the passerby's attention, the individual could come up closer to the poster to read more about who Don Norman is in the smaller text.

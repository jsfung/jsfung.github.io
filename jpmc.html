    My Role
    Design Lead

    Project Type
    Web App Redesign
    2 months | January 2017 - February 2017

    Teammate
    Mary O'Brien

    At JPMorgan Chase & Co, we created a redesign proposal to simplify the navigation of my team's web app supporting Asset Management, which is accessed by 2,500+ global business users. It is a workflow tool where people are assigned cases based on problems with accounts.

    There is a limit to what information I can share of the designs, but I can talk about my process and what I learned about designing with limited access to users.

    Challenge

    How Can I Design For Users That I Cannot Interact With?

    Due to issues relating to privacy of data, development teams are unable to commmunicate with employees of the firm that deal with PI data.

    We were given the requirement of making navigating away from the homepage a more visually pleasing experience.


    Solution

    Adapt the Process to Use Available Resources

    I recognized that we had access to two groups of people: Members of my team who are experts on the application and random employees on the floor who have never touched the application before.

    We asked these two groups to roleplay and participate in cognitive walkthroughs during testing in order to get a better picture of my users, influencing my design


    Final Redesign

    We focused on redesigning the homepage, case grid, and search pages. Dummy data is displayed in the screens below and some labels have been removed.


    Homepage

    Before
    No clear hierarchy, too many menu options, and lack of styling with margins/padding made the page clunky and hard to navigate.

    After
    We created hierarchy by revealing items in an accordion, which allows quick skimming of case statuses with visual dashboard. We also reduced visible menu items to only the most-used items.

    *Key business requirement was to introduce the dashboard to the homepage.


    Create Menu

    Before
    So many options in the flyout menu made looking for a specific case category difficult.

    After
    We displayed all category groupings and case categories on a single page for easier skim and search.


    Search

    Before
    The clunky-looking interface disrupts the experience in making the application look less clean.

    After
    Introducing a simple styling cleaned up the page, making it more modern and pleasing for users to use.


    Design + Research Process

    Designing For Three Types of Users

    From the requirements, I recognized that there were three groups of users.

    I asked my manager to walk through a task on the current application, posing as each user. As he performed the tasks, I asked why he selected the items he did and which items on the screen were important or missing. This gave me more visibility into the motives of each user.

    __________

    Personalized Screens for Each User

    In the current state, every user sees the same pages. Having the same screen for each user doesn't make sense becase they want to do and see different things. From listing out use cases for each user, we saw what components of the application mattered most.

    __________

    Design + Feedback

    We had a whiteboarding session where we created different ways of representing the needed information on a screen via paper prototypes before jumping into 3 iterations of mi-fi prototyping in PowerPoint, and 3 iterations of hi-fi prototyping in Figma. Between iterations, we tested the designs with 6 roleplaying users.

    Current State

    Project Hand-off

    The redesign proposal has been handed off to our team's business analyst to present to business users.
